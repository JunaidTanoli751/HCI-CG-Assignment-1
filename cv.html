Key Principles and Concepts

Norman's Seven Principles of Design

  1. Discoverability: Users should be able to discover what actions are possible
  2. Feedback: Information about the result of an action should be immediate and clear
  3. Conceptual Model: Design should communicate a clear mental model
  4. Affordances: Design attributes that suggest how an object should be used
  5. Signifiers: Perceivable indicators of affordances
  6. Mappings: Relationship between controls and their effects
  7. Constraints: Limiting possible actions to prevent errors
